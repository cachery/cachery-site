The problem

Your AI usage is growing. So is the bill.

LLM spend scales with usage and eats your gross margin. The obvious fix — routing to cheaper models or caching answers — quietly risks shipping worse answers to your users. Most tools cut cost and just hope quality holds. Cachet measures it.

The product

Meet Cachet

Point your app at one endpoint (OpenAI-compatible — change one base URL). It runs in your environment, and three things make it safe to trust:

1 · Runs in your environment

Your data never leaves

Prompts and answers stay inside your own infrastructure — no third party sees your traffic. Route a tier to a local model via Ollama for fully on-prem, $0 inference. Drop-in OpenAI-compatible; deploy with Docker.

2 · Verified, not predicted

A quality floor per request

Hosted routers guess which model will be best. Cachet drafts cheap, verifies the actual answer, and escalates to a stronger model only when it isn't good enough — a measured floor on every request, not a bet. Repeats are then served from a quality-gated cache, never worse than a fresh route.

3 · Cost + governance in one

The whole control plane

Not just routing: a semantic cache (repeats served free at proven quality), per-team budgets and spend attribution, PII guardrails, and a continuous quality probe that grades whether cheaper answers stayed correct and helpful — all self-hosted.

The difference

Your traffic is your business. Keep it that way.

Sending every prompt to a hosted router means a third party sees all of it — and you're trusting their prediction of quality with no per-request safety net. For healthcare, fintech, legal, or anyone under a compliance regime, that's a non-starter.

Cachet inverts it: the gateway runs in your environment, the cache and the spend data stay with you, and every cheap answer is verified before it's served. You get the savings of routing and the savings of caching, with quality you can audit — without handing your data to anyone.

Honest notes: ~77% is cost-routing alone on all-unique traffic (the worst case); cached repeats are free, so blended savings climb past 90% as traffic repeats. About 12% of the benchmark questions can't be answered factually without the customer's own product data — an all-premium model only hedges — so the full-set 86% understates the routing quality; on the answerable set it's ~92% (≈93% of what the all-premium Opus 4.8 baseline achieves). More than savings

The control plane for your LLM traffic

Cost governance

Budgets & attribution

Guardrails & compliance

PII never leaves

Detect and redact sensitive data (emails, SSNs, cards) before a prompt reaches any model — or block it outright. Every action is audit-logged.

Quality assurance

Catch regressions

Continuous "is the cheap model still good enough?" checks with a quality floor and alerts — so cost savings can never silently degrade your answers.

Vendor-agnostic

Gemini, Claude, and OpenAI — one gateway

Most teams already run more than one model provider. Cachet sits in front of all of them through one OpenAI-compatible endpoint — so the cache, budgets, guardrails, and quality checks apply to every vendor's traffic, not just one.

One endpoint, every vendor

Cache across providers

Keep using any model — Claude, GPT, Gemini, or open models. A repeated question is served free whichever vendor would have answered it, and all your spend lands on one bill with per-team attribution.

Cross-provider routing

Cheapest model that's good enough

Each rung of the cascade — draft, mid, premium — can be any vendor's model. Mix freely (say, a Gemini Flash draft and a Claude premium backstop) and let the gateway serve the cheapest model that clears the quality bar.

No lock-in

Swap models in config

Change a provider or rebalance tiers without touching your app. As pricing and models shift you re-route in one line — and the same measured-quality checks keep cost cuts honest across every provider.
