The problem

Your AI usage is growing. So is the bill.

LLM spend scales with usage and eats your gross margin. The obvious fix — routing to cheaper models or caching answers — quietly risks shipping worse answers to your users. Most tools cut cost and just hope quality holds. Cachery measures it.

The product

Meet Cachery

Point your app at one endpoint (OpenAI-compatible — change one base URL). It runs in your environment, and three things make it safe to trust:

1 · Runs in your environment

Your data never leaves

Prompts and answers stay inside your own infrastructure — no third party sees your traffic. Route a tier to a local model via Ollama for fully on-prem, $0 inference. Drop-in OpenAI-compatible; deploy with Docker.

2 · Verified, not predicted

A quality floor per request

Hosted routers guess which model will be best. Cachery drafts cheap, verifies the actual answer, and escalates to a stronger model only when it isn't good enough — a measured floor on every request, not a bet. Repeats are then served from a quality-gated cache, never worse than a fresh route.

3 · Cost + governance in one

The whole control plane

Not just routing: a semantic cache (repeats served free at proven quality), per-team budgets and spend attribution, PII guardrails, and a continuous quality probe that grades whether cheaper answers stayed correct and helpful — all self-hosted.

The difference

Your traffic is your business. Keep it that way.

Sending every prompt to a hosted router means a third party sees all of it — and you're trusting their prediction of quality with no per-request safety net. For healthcare, fintech, legal, or anyone under a compliance regime, that's a non-starter.

Cachery inverts it: the gateway runs in your environment, the cache and the spend data stay with you, and every cheap answer is verified before it's served. You get the savings of routing and the savings of caching, with quality you can audit — without handing your data to anyone.

Who it's for

Built for AI product teams

Best fit: companies whose product is an AI assistant, support bot, or copilot with high-volume, repetitive, customer-facing questions — where the LLM bill is a top cost line and a wrong answer actually matters. That's exactly where caching saves the most and where "measured quality" is worth paying for.

Less of a fit: ultra-low-latency or real-time autocomplete, where every millisecond dominates — verifying an answer before serving trades a little latency for a correctness guarantee. Worth it when a wrong answer costs more than ~200ms; not when it doesn't.

FAQ

Good questions

How hard is it to integrate?
It's an OpenAI-compatible endpoint — typically a one-line base-URL change.
Can I see the savings on my own traffic before integrating?
Yes — shadow mode. Mirror a copy of your live requests and Cachery measures what it would have cost and at what quality, out-of-band, without serving those answers. Your production path doesn't change, and the audit is graded by an independent judge on your own prompts.
Will it serve a wrong cached answer for a similar question?
No — a reranker confirms true equivalence before any cache hit, and we measure the false-hit rate (near zero in our benchmark).
What stage is the product?
A working, audited product that's now self-hosted and production-hardened — Docker deploy, automated tests in CI, plus reliability (failover, circuit breakers) and observability (metrics, structured logs) built in. We're onboarding design partners; we're upfront that formal certifications (e.g. SOC 2) are still ahead of us.
Which models do you support?
Provider-agnostic by design — premium and low-cost tiers are configurable, with automatic failover between them built in.
