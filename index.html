The problem

Your AI usage is growing. So is the bill.

LLM spend scales with usage and eats your gross margin. The obvious fix — routing to cheaper models or caching answers — quietly risks shipping worse answers to your users. Most tools cut cost and just hope quality holds. Cachet measures it.

The product

Meet Cachet

Point your app at one endpoint (OpenAI-compatible — change one base URL). Three layers do the work:

1 · Semantic cache

Repeats are free

Identical and reworded questions are served from cache for $0 — matched by meaning, with a reranker so a similar-but-different question never gets the wrong answer.

2 · Quality-gated routing

Cheap first, verified

New questions are drafted by a low-cost model, checked, and escalated to a premium model only when the draft isn't good enough. Precision-sensitive prompts skip to premium.

3 · Measured quality

Savings you can trust

A built-in evaluation harness continuously grades whether cheaper answers stayed correct and helpful — so cost cuts can't silently degrade your product.

FAQ

Good questions

How hard is it to integrate?
It's an OpenAI-compatible endpoint — typically a one-line base-URL change.
Will it serve a wrong cached answer for a similar question?
No — a reranker confirms true equivalence before any cache hit, and we measure the false-hit rate (near zero in our benchmark).
What stage is the product?
A working, audited product that's now self-hosted and production-hardened — Docker deploy, automated tests in CI, plus reliability (failover, circuit breakers) and observability (metrics, structured logs) built in. We're onboarding design partners; we're upfront that formal certifications (e.g. SOC 2) are still ahead of us.
Which models do you support?
Provider-agnostic by design — premium and low-cost tiers are configurable, with automatic failover between them built in.
